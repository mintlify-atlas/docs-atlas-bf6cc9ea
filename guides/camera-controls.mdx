## Overview

Cinema Studio in Open Higgsfield AI provides professional-grade camera controls that transform your prompts into cinematic imagery. By selecting cameras, lenses, focal lengths, and aperture settings, you can achieve Hollywood-quality visual styles.

## How Camera Controls Work

Cinema Studio uses the **Nano Banana** and **Nano Banana Pro** models with advanced prompt engineering. Your camera selections are automatically translated into technical photography terms and appended to your prompt:

```javascript
// From src/lib/promptUtils.js:48-74
export function buildNanoBananaPrompt(basePrompt, camera, lens, focalLength, aperture) {
    const parts = [
        basePrompt,
        `shot on a ${cameraDesc}`,
        `using a ${lensDesc} at ${focalLength}mm (${perspective})`,
        `aperture ${aperture}`,
        depthEffect,
        "cinematic lighting",
        "natural color science",
        "high dynamic range",
        "professional photography, ultra-detailed, 8K resolution"
    ];
    
    return parts.join(", ");
}
```

<Info>
**Example Transformation:**

Your prompt: `"A lone astronaut on Mars"`

With Cinema Studio:
`"A lone astronaut on Mars, shot on a full-frame digital cinema camera, using a premium modern prime lens at 50mm (standard portrait perspective), aperture f/1.4, shallow depth of field, creamy bokeh, cinematic lighting, natural color science, high dynamic range, professional photography, ultra-detailed, 8K resolution"`
</Info>

## Focal Length Guide

Focal length controls perspective and field of view. Cinema Studio offers 6 focal lengths:

<Steps>
  <Step title="8mm - Ultra-Wide Perspective">

    **Field of View:** Extreme (180°+)

    **Characteristics:**
    - Dramatic distortion
    - Exaggerated depth
    - Curved lines at edges
    - Fish-eye effect

    **Use Cases:**
    - Action sequences (GoPro-style)
    - Skateboarding/sports
    - Immersive POV shots

    **Emotional Impact:** Energetic, intense, immersive
  </Step>

  <Step title="14mm - Wide-Angle Perspective">

    **Field of View:** Very Wide (~114°)

    **Emotional Impact:** Grand, epic, spacious

  <Step title="24mm - Wide-Angle Dynamic Perspective">

## Aperture and Depth of Field

Aperture controls depth of field and bokeh quality. Cinema Studio offers 3 apertures:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="f/1.4 - Wide Open">
    **Technical Prompt:** `"shallow depth of field, creamy bokeh"`

    ### Characteristics
    - **Depth of Field:** Extremely shallow
    - **Bokeh:** Smooth, creamy, out-of-focus backgrounds
    - **Light Gathering:** Maximum (best for low light)
    - **Subject Isolation:** Strong

    ### Visual Effects
    - Blurred backgrounds
    - Soft, dreamy quality
    - Pronounced subject separation
    - Large, smooth bokeh circles

    ### Use Cases
    - Portraits (dramatic subject isolation)
    - Low-light scenes
    - Cinematic close-ups
    - Romantic/dreamy moods
    - Product photography

    ### Emotional Impact
    Intimate, focused, dreamlike, professional

    ### Technical Note
    **Bokeh Shape:** Circular, smooth ("creamy bokeh" from prompt)

    <Tip>
    **Pro Tip:** Combine f/1.4 with 50mm or 85mm focal length for maximum subject isolation and beautiful bokeh.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="f/4 - Balanced">
    **Technical Prompt:** `"balanced depth of field"`

    ### Characteristics
    - **Depth of Field:** Moderate
    - **Bokeh:** Present but less pronounced
    - **Sharpness:** Excellent across frame
    - **Subject + Context:** Both visible

    ### Visual Effects
    - Subject sharp, background soft but recognizable
    - Balanced composition
    - Good edge-to-edge sharpness
    - Natural-looking depth

    ### Use Cases
    - Environmental portraits
    - Street photography
    - Travel photography
    - Cinematic medium shots
    - Group shots (2-3 people)

    ### Emotional Impact
    Natural, balanced, storytelling, context-aware

    ### Technical Note
    **Sweet Spot:** Many lenses perform best at f/4 (sharpest with good bokeh)

    <Info>
    **Versatility:** f/4 is the most versatile aperture - sharp subject with visible but blurred background context.
    </Info>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="f/11 - Deep Focus">
    **Technical Prompt:** `"deep focus clarity, sharp foreground to background"`

    ### Characteristics
    - **Depth of Field:** Very deep
    - **Bokeh:** Minimal/none
    - **Sharpness:** Entire scene in focus
    - **Detail:** Maximum throughout frame

    ### Visual Effects
    - Foreground to background sharp
    - Maximum detail everywhere
    - No bokeh/blur
    - Landscape-style clarity

    ### Use Cases
    - Landscapes
    - Architecture
    - Group shots (large groups)
    - Establishing shots
    - Documentary/journalistic work
    - Technical/product photography

    ### Emotional Impact
    Epic, grand, detail-oriented, observational

    ### Technical Note
    **Diffraction Limit:** f/11 balances deep DOF with diffraction (going beyond f/11 reduces sharpness)

    <Warning>
    **Avoid for Portraits:** f/11 creates busy, distracting backgrounds. Use f/1.4 or f/4 instead.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## UI Interaction Details

The Cinema Studio interface uses scrollable wheels for control selection:

- **Drag to scroll** through options
- **Click item** to jump directly
- **Active item** highlighted with primary color and scale effect
- **Smooth animations** for professional feel

From `CameraControls.js:78-110`, the component implements touch-friendly drag scrolling:

```javascript
list.addEventListener('mousedown', (e) => {
    isDown = true;
    startY = e.pageY - list.offsetTop;
    scrollTop = list.scrollTop;
});

list.addEventListener('mousemove', (e) => {
    if (!isDown) return;
    const y = e.pageY - list.offsetTop;
    const walk = (y - startY) * 1.5; // Scroll speed multiplier
    list.scrollTop = scrollTop - walk;
});
```

## Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start with Focal Length">
    Choose focal length based on your subject:
    - Wide (8-24mm) for environments
    - Normal (35-50mm) for natural looks
    - Telephoto (85mm) for portraits
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Camera Format">
    Match camera to desired aesthetic:
    - Film cameras for organic/vintage
    - Digital cameras for clean/modern
    - Large format for luxury/bokeh
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose Lens Character">
    Pick lens based on mood:
    - Modern primes for commercial
    - Vintage/warm for nostalgic
    - Anamorphic for epic/sci-fi
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Aperture for Depth">
    Control focus:
    - f/1.4 for isolation/bokeh
    - f/4 for balance
    - f/11 for sharpness throughout
  </Step>

  <Step title="Iterate and Refine">
    Generate multiple variations:
    - Test different focal lengths
    - Compare aperture settings
    - Mix camera/lens combinations
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
**Pro Workflow:** Start with defaults (35mm, f/1.4, Full-Frame Cine, Premium Modern Prime), generate, then adjust one parameter at a time to see its effect.
</Tip>
